The I Ching (or Site of Changes)

The I Ching is an ancient Chinese oracular text.
For centuries, it has provided direction to those who seek guidance.
Traditionally one consults the oracle by tossing six sets of three coins.
Each coin tossed yields a value, and each set's total value reveals an open or closed line.
The six resulting lines comprise the hexagram, a visual reading.
There are 64 possible hexagrams, each with its own accompanying interpretation.

All interpretation texts are excerpted and adapted from Richard Wilhelm's 1950 German translation of the Chinese text, translated into English by Cary F. Baynes and published by Princeton University Press in 1977.
This site presents a simplified reading of the I Ching. For more information about the I Ching and for more accurate reading methodology, please consult Wilhelm's comprehensive text. As displayed here, Wilhelm's texts have been edited wherever possible for gender neutrality, which may change the nature of the interpretation.
